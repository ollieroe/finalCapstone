Exercise

The aim of exercise is both to have fun or to keep you healthy. If it's not one of the two, then you should probably be getting paid for doing it (charity counts!)♥

Important considerations for choosing something to do

  • Do you like it? trying to go for a 10 mile run after a few years of not running might make you want to never run again. Going for a gentle 30 second run / 1 minute rest might make you think it's not that bad. If it's pure exercise you want, lower the hurdle, make it easier and stop when you like. ♥
  • If the gym/running and general cardio isn't your up to tea. Try some adventuring to a place you've never been. Try a sport you've never tried or try something that you wouldn't immedaitely think of as exercise. If it gets you moving, that's the main thing. ♥
  • If you have a habit of not continuing with things. Try to find a group to help you. It can be a better environment that takes away from the feeling of needing to exercise and replacing it with wanting to see those people. They can also take an active interest and to some extent hold you accountable, increasing the likelihood of regular attendance. ♥
  • Do something fun. If you don't like it, try something else. If you don't like that, try again. It may take some effort but there should be something you'll find interesting enough to keep at for a while. ♥
